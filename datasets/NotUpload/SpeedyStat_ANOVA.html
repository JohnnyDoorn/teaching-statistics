The assumption of normality can be assessed in several ways. There exist specific hypothesis tests that test the hypothesis that the data are normally distributed (null hypothesis), versus the hypothesis that the data are non-normally distributed (alternative hypothesis). To assess the normality in this way, we can use the Descriptives module in JASP, and tick the box "Shapiro-Wilk test". To assess the normality of each group in the design individually, we can Split by the categorical variable. Following these steps gives table below with the Shapiro-Wilk test-statistic, as well as its associated p-value. For none of the groups the null hypothesis can be rejected for alpha of 0.05.

However, these hypothesis tests are not ideal because they make it very tempting to reason in a binary way; when your Shapiro p-value equals 0.055, are you completely safe from non-normality? And a is this so different than if you would have a p-value of 0.045? In the following analysis, we will conduct a full ANOVA and show an alternative way to assess the normality of the data.

Assumption Checks

The homogeneity of variance assumption can also be assessed by either a hypothesis test, or a more fluid way. We can either look at the p-value of the Levene's test (below), or by "eye-balling" the standard deviations in the descriptives table above. The latter way is definitely more subjective, but allows more of a gradual assessment of the equality of variances (as opposed to a strict yes/no decision).

Similarly, we can assess the normality assumption by means of the Q-Q plot below. Here, the residuals of the model are compared to the normal distribution: the more points are on the line, the better the assumption of normality holds. In ANOVA, we can asesss normality either by looking the data itself, or at the residuals (prediction errors of the model). Field explains this normality in more detail on p326 (especially misconception box 6.1).

Exam note: in a classroom setting things need to be sharply defined, and having a teacher saying "just eyeball it and do what feels right!" is not very useful in an exam setting. Therefore, on the exam we will only test assumptions by means of the hypothesis tests (but you do need to know that normality can be assessed by Q-Q plots).
